Pedro Doll

Welcome to my website!

I am a geologist based in Aotearoa (New Zealand), specialised in volcano geochronology. I have recently finished my PhD at the University of Canterbury, in which I used cosmogenic 3He, paleomagnetic dating, and lava flow volume estimations to study the postglacial constructive history of Mt Ruapehu. You can find my full thesis here.

I am interested in understanding how volcanic systems evolve over time, and how their activity impacts the landscape, society, and environment. I enjoy fieldwork and laboratory work, and programming data analysis algorithms to interpret geochemical, geophysical, and geomorphological data. I am also curious about dating methods and their applications to lava flows and other volcanic deposits, and I am open to collaborate in projects pushing the boundaries of dating techniques.

Currently, I am working as an application specialist in the cloud engineering department at Seequent, one of the leading companies in the geological modeling software industy.
